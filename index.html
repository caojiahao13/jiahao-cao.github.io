About

Statistical methodology for structured, uncertain, and evolving data.

My research centers on Bayesian statistics, statistical methodology, theory, computation, and machine learning. I am interested in building interpretable and computationally scalable methods for data that are spatially indexed, temporally evolving, high-dimensional, functional, constrained, or affected by measurement and privacy-related uncertainty.

Research

Methodological Interests

  • Bayesian and nonparametric modeling, including Bayesian additive regression trees, mixture models, hierarchical models, Bayesian networks, and scalable posterior computation.
  • Spatial and spatio-temporal statistics, including Gaussian processes, log-Gaussian Cox processes, small-area estimation, disease mapping, and network-based spatial models.
  • Complex structured data analysis, including compositional data, functional data, point process data, high-dimensional regression, and constrained or shape-preserving inference.
  • Dynamic modeling and latent structure discovery, including clustering, community detection, nonlinear time series, and change point detection.

Application Areas

I use these methods in applied settings where uncertainty, structure, and computation matter, including public health and epidemiology, environmental and spatial health studies, sports analytics, urban systems, and financial or economic functional time series.
